Profile

"A geek philosopher", these are the three words that best describe me. What does it mean? I am passionate about understanding and learning in general and about coding and data in particular.

In an ideal professional situation, some colleagues and I would be standing in front of a white board, seeking for innovation or writing down problems, and brainstorming about possible solutions that we could implement and test; but more importantly, going back to step one to either find alternative solutions or new ways of innovating. Why is it more important to me? Because it offers opportunities to consider and/or adopt new perspectives and to learn new tools or techniques in order to solve problems.

Positions
2014-Now
Postdoctoral researcher in cognitive neurosciences
Donders Institute for Brain, Cognition & Behaviour (Nijmegen, the Netherlands)
During these past 4 years in the lab of Pr. Pieter Medendorp, I combined psychophysical experiments using eye and body-motion tracking techniques as well as computational modeling to investigate how the brain combines multisensory information (e.g. visuo-vestibular) to keep track of spatial positions in dynamic environments. Beside my research, I have also been supervising Ph.D candidates and undergraduate students, and I have been actively reviewing manuscripts for several scientific journals.
2011-2014
Ph.D candidate in Cognitive psychology
Laboratoire Psychologie de la Perception (University Paris Descartes, Paris, France)
Under the supervision of Pr. Patrick Cavanagh, I conducted psychophysical research combined with eye and hand movements tracking techniques to investigate the visuomotor and visual memory processes underlying drawing accuracy. Next to this activity, I taught several courses in a variety of fields (Moral philosophy, history of psychology and epistemology, statistics and programming and cognitive sciences) at the University Paris Descartes and Ecole Normale Supérieure (Paris, Ulm). I also did some consulting for teachers in art education and I have been involved in an interdisciplinary artistic project.
